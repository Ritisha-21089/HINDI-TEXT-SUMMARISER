Motivation

The goal behind creating a Hindi summarization application is to bridge the gap between the vast amount of information available in Hindi and the need for quick, efficient comprehension. By developing such a tool, the aim is to empower users to extract key insights and main points from Hindi texts rapidly, saving time and effort. 

 

 

Problem Statement

The task requires the creation of an algorithm to produce fixed-length, abstractive summaries for a dataset of articles characterized by their use of code-mixing and script-mixing. This requirement stands apart from traditional summarization challenges, which typically involve single-language text inputs. The proposed solution must navigate the linguistic intricacies presented by the mixed-language and script environment to deliver clear and accurate summaries.

Novelty

Our model introduces a novel approach by integrating several advanced functionalities that go beyond mere text summarization. In addition to generating concise summaries of Hindi text, it is equipped with a Named Entity Recognition (NER) component that extracts key terms and entities from the input, enhancing the utility and contextual relevance of the summaries. Moreover, the model embraces multi-modality by incorporating Optical Character Recognition (OCR) capabilities, which allows it to process and interpret not only digital texts but also scanned documents and images containing text. This multi-faceted approach enables a more versatile application, catering to a broader range of input formats and delivering richer, more informative output.

Dataset 

The Ilsum Hindi Train dataset, comprising 21,225 entries, was employed to fine-tune existing models and identify the top-performing models for integration into our web application.

Model

Our summarization tool utilizes two specialized models: a fine-tuned Google mT5 base model for generating summaries and a BI-LSTM CRF model leveraging fastText word embeddings for named entity recognition (NER).

Performance

Our models capture semantic information and are optimized for abstractive summarization. They effectively identify key named entities, enabling users to quickly grasp the main points of the text.
